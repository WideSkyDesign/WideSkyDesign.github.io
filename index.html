The Beach

Cleethorpe has a long, sandy beach enjoyed by holidaymakers.

Dolphin Hotel

The Dolphin Hotel was Cleethorpes' most well-known hotel.

Promenade

The spacious Promenade was built above the Sea Wall in 1885.

Sea Wall

The Sea Wall was built to protect the Cleethorpes coastline.

Railway Station

The Railway Station has welcomed millions of holidaymakers.

Mermaid Cafe

The Mermaid Café was once the Railway Station waiting room.

Empire Theatre

The Empire has had many different uses through the years.

The Pier

The Pier was the heart of Cleethorpes' seaside entertainment.

Cliff Gardens

Winding paths lead through Cliff Gardens up to Ross Castle.

Grotto Cafe

The Grotto Café was a popular stopping place on the sea front.

Ross Castle

When is a castle not a castle?

Alexandra Road

Alexandra Road has fine villas with wrought iron balconies.

Fish Hatchery

The Fish Hatchery was Watkin's idea to conserve fish stocks.

Coliseum Picture Theatre

The Coliseum was one of Cleethorpes' most popular cinemas.

Kingsway Bathing Pool

Cleethorpes Bathing Pool was once the UK's biggest outdoor pool.

Light Railway

The Light Railway runs with restored miniature steam engines.

Recycling Sculpture

The three Recycling Sculptures help to keep the seafront clean.

The Fitties

The Fitties are home to many birds, year-round and temporary.
